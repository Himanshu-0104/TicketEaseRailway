1. Indian Railways is the world’s fourth largest and Largest Rail Network in Asia.

Indian railways has a route of 67,415 kilometres making it the largest rail network in Asia and fourth-largest in the world. It is also the world’s second-largest railway network operated under single management. It runs 12,617 trains to carry more than 23 million passengers per day. During this journey, it connects more than 7,172 stations across India.

2. The Indian Railways is the world’s eighth largest workforce with 1.4 million employees.

The Indian Railways is the world’s eighth largest employer with a total of 1.4 million employees. It is one of the largest employers in the world, after the Chinese People’s Liberation Army, Walmart, McDonald’s, and U.S. Department of Defense.

3. UNESCO includes two historical railway elements in the World Heritage site list - The Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus, and the Indian Mountain Railways.

The UNESCO World Heritage Site list includes two railway elements. Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus is one of them, and it is the busiest railway station in India. The Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus was built in 1887 and has been a part of Mumbai ever since. It was recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 2018 for its architectural significance and its role as an important cultural hub for Mumbaikars.

4. Indian Railways has five luxury trains.

These are: Royal Rajasthan on Wheels, Palace on Wheels (Rajasthan), The Golden Chariot (Karnataka and Goa), The Maharajas’ Express (begins in Delhi but the itinerary differs) and The Deccan Odyssey (begins in Maharashtra but itinerary differs).

5. The Fairy Queen is the oldest working steam locomotive in India that's still in use.

The Fairy Queen is a small, narrow gauge locomotive that was built in 1855 by Kitson and Co. in Leeds, England. It has been in use since 1878. . It has been used continuously since 1878 when it was brought to India from the UK for service on the Darjeeling Himalayan Railway.

6. Railway Line 1.5 times of earth.

If the tracks of Indian railways were to be laid out, they would circle the earth almost 1.5 times.

7. Longest Tunnel in India .

The longest tunnel in the country is Pir Panjal Railway tunnel in Jammu and Kashmir which is 11.25 kms long. The tunnel was constructed to connect Jammu and Kashmir to the mainland of India. It is one of the most important railway tunnels in India as it connects the state with the rest of the country.The construction of this tunnel was started on September 20, 2011 and completed on November 30, 2017. The first train entered this railway tunnel on April 2nd 2018 which was a historic moment for both Indians and Pakistan.

8.The busiest junction in the country is Howrah Junction in Kolkata.

Howrah junction is one of the busiest railway junctions in India.The junction has 974 trains stopping there daily, which makes it one of the busiest junctions in the country. It connects rail traffic between Kolkata and Eastern India to Western India and vice versa.

9. Largest Rail Museum in Asia

India has eight Railway Museums – in Delhi, Pune, Kanpur, Mysore, Kolkata, Chennai, Ghum and Tiruchirappalli. Out of these, the National Rail Museum at New Delhi is one of the most popular tourist attractions in India and largest museum in Asia.

10. Highest Rail Bridges in the World.

The under-construction Chenab railway bridge in Reasi district, Jammu & Kashmir will be the tallest railway bridge in the world. Five times taller than Qutab Minar.
